# Profile: Project Reveal (remodel / build / renovation)

The finished-space reveal — a remodel, a custom build, a renovation. The deliverable is an **ordered carousel** that walks a viewer through the space as a story. Order is load-bearing here: this is the one profile where sequence is the whole point.

## What "best" means

A frame earns a slot if it either (a) advances the spatial story or (b) is clean enough to stand alone as a text-over background later. Favor frames with depth, light, and a clear subject over busy or subjectless ones.

## Order — the narrative walk

Open strong, establish wide, move through the space zone by zone, close on a pull-back or lifestyle frame. For a kitchen that's often: **wide establishing → feature wall → cooking zone → sink/window → storage details → materials/hardware → lifestyle pull-back.** Other rooms have their own zones, but the shape is the same: orient, walk, detail, land.

- **No two adjacent slides are near-identical angles.** This is the cardinal rule of the ordered profile. Two sink shots back to back read as filler — pick the better one.
- **Vary the shot type across the sequence.** Don't stack five wides then five details; interleave so the swipe keeps surprising.
- **Open on a wide, not a tight detail.** Slide 1 sets the room; macros come later.

## Target count

Usually given ("top 20", "best 12"). Instagram caps a carousel at **20 slides**. If you open with a split-hero (one landscape photo split into two seamless 4:5 panels — `split-hero.sh`), that hero eats **two slides**, so 20 slides = 19 photos. Do that math before promising a number.

## Dedup

Moderate. Keeping two angles of a genuine hero feature is fine if they're separated in the sequence and each adds something (e.g. a fridge-panel-closed wide and a panel-open detail). Cut true repeats.

## Flag for the editor

- Orientation: which frames are vertical vs the landscape hero (the hero must stay landscape for the split).
- Construction leftovers visible in raws (tape, laser lines, protective film) — note them for spot-heal.
- Exposure consistency across the set (interiors shot in mixed light often need a uniform lift).

## Output

Slot-numbered staging in posting order (`stage-picks.sh`), edit-always clients stage to a `_to-edit` folder. After edits land, optional split-hero rebuild + the rest as `03.jpg…`. Hand the slide design to `carousel-slides` if branded text slides are wanted instead of full-bleed photos.

## Reusable background library

These sets often double as a text-over-background library. When that's the goal, lean toward clean, composed frames with negative space over tight busy crops, and tell the user where the edited finals should live permanently (e.g. an `Assets/Backgrounds/<project>/` folder).
