# Profile: Event (corporate / brand / conference)

A brand or corporate event — a conference, a product launch, a gala, a grand opening. The deliverable is usually a **recap**: a tight set of hero moments for a carousel, a highlight reel, or a wrap-up post. Fewer frames than a wedding, more curated than a gallery.

## Segments

- **Stage / keynote** (speakers, the moment of the announcement, slides behind them)
- **The room / audience** (engaged crowd, full-house wides, reactions)
- **Details & branding** (signage, logo placements, the product, swag, food styling)
- **Candids & networking** (people talking, laughing, the human texture)
- **VIP / key people** (named execs, sponsors, the guest of honor — coverage matters here)

## What "best" means

Frames that make the event look *well-attended, energetic, and on-brand.* For corporate clients, **visible branding and a full room are assets** — a hero shot with the logo clean in frame and an engaged crowd does more than a prettier but anonymous one. Reaction and energy beat static posing.

## Order

Depends on the deliverable. For a **recap carousel** it's a light narrative (open on the marquee moment or a full-room wide → speakers → details → candids → a strong closing crowd/energy frame), so the reveal-style "no adjacent duplicate angles" rule applies. For a raw **highlight dump** to the client, group by segment, order loosely chronological.

## Target count

Ask. Defaults to offer: **recap carousel ~10–20**, **highlight set for the client ~30–60**. If it's feeding a reel, the count is looser — pick the strongest 20–40 stills and note which are motion/energy frames.

## Dedup

Moderate-to-aggressive. You need *variety of moment*, not ten frames of the same speaker. One strong frame per speaker/segment, then spread.

## Flag

- **Recognizable faces & releases** — corporate events often have consent constraints; surface any frame headed to public marketing that features identifiable non-staff guests.
- **Branding legibility** — note frames where the logo/signage is clean and usable vs cut off.
- **Bad-look frames** — half-empty rooms, mid-bite, awkward gestures, anyone looking unflattering. Cut early; these damage the client.

## Output

Slot-numbered staging. For a recap carousel, in posting order. For a client highlight set, grouped by segment. Hand carousel design to `carousel-slides` if branded slides are wanted.
