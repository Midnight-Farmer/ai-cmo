# Profile: Real Estate / Interiors (listing or interiors shoot)

A property shoot — a listing, an interiors portfolio, an architectural set. The deliverable prizes **complete coverage** over editorial flair: a buyer (or the MLS) needs to see *every* space, each in its single strongest frame. This is the profile where **completeness beats variety** — a gorgeous third angle of the kitchen is worth less than the one decent frame of the bathroom you'd otherwise skip.

## What "best" means

The single cleanest, most spacious-looking frame **per room/space**. Favor: straight verticals (walls not keystoning), bright and even exposure, wide enough to show the room's proportion, minimal clutter. One hero per space, then stop.

## Order — the walkthrough

Order as a buyer would tour the property:

**Exterior / curb → entry → main living → kitchen → dining → primary bedroom → primary bath → secondary bedrooms → secondary baths → bonus/flex spaces → outdoor / yard → amenities / views.**

This isn't a dramatic narrative; it's a logical tour. Lead with the strongest exterior or the hero living space (listings often want the best foot forward as the thumbnail).

## Target count

Often "one per room" or a platform cap (many MLS feeds want ~20–40). Ask if unspecified. If a room genuinely has two sellable angles (e.g. a great-room from both ends), two is fine — but coverage of *all* rooms comes first.

## Orientation matters

MLS and most listing platforms strongly prefer **horizontal** frames. Flag verticals; note where only a vertical exists for a room so the user can decide.

## Dedup

Aggressive — one best per space. The failure mode here is five kitchen angles and no laundry room. After selecting, **list the rooms covered and any room with no usable frame** so gaps are visible before the shoot is considered done.

## Flag

- **Coverage gaps** — any room/space with no usable frame. This is the most important flag in this profile.
- Keystoning / leaning verticals (fixable in edit, but note severity).
- Clutter, visible cords, open toilet lids, reflections of the photographer in mirrors.
- Twilight / exterior hero frames if present (listings love them as the lead image).

## Output

Slot-numbered staging in walkthrough order. Include a short coverage checklist (room → chosen frame, or "GAP") alongside the picks.
